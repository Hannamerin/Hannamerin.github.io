About Me

\

I am a Ph.D. candidate researching refugee narratives and literary sociology with a focus on Australian offshore detention literature. My current work develops Manus Prison Theory, a conceptual model rooted in the lived experiences of Manus Island detainees. I explore how refugee-authored texts interrogate legality, voice, identity, and carceral systems. I am NET-qualified (June 2019 & December 2024) and have prior experience as an Assistant Professor and Content Analyst.
