About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate researching refugee narratives and literary sociology, with a focus on Australia’s offshore detention regime. My work develops Manus Prison Theory, a conceptual model rooted in the lived experiences of Manus Island detainees. I explore how refugee-authored texts interrogate legality, identity, carceral structures, and the limits of justice. I am UGC-NET qualified and have served as Assistant Professor and Content Analyst.
